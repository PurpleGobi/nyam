# Food & Wine Social App: Social Features Research

> Research Date: 2026-03-21
> Purpose: Inform Nyam v2 profile page and social feature design

---

## Part 1: App-by-App Analysis

### 1. Vivino (Wine Social) — 60M+ users

**Profile Page Shows:**
- Wine cellar (tasted / want to try / owned)
- Taste Profile with flavor preferences (auto-generated from ratings)
- Match percentage for each wine (personalized)
- Ratings history, tasting notes
- Friend list (Facebook/Google/contact import)

**Most Engaging Social Features:**
- Label scanning → instant review lookup (2M scans/day)
- Friend feed showing what friends are drinking
- Personalized match % per wine
- Community tasting notes (crowdsourced)

**Activity Feed:**
- Friend feed: chronological stream of friends' wine check-ins
- Wine-centric rather than social-centric

**Gamification:**
- Taste Profile that evolves over time — users enjoy watching it change
- No badge system (kept simple)
- "Wine Match %" acts as soft gamification

**User Complaints:**
- Scanner unreliability after updates causes rage
- Paywall creep — features that were free now behind Premium ($)
- Data loss (cellar/rating history disappearing)
- Customer service is "non-existent"
- Excessive ads post-commercialization

**What Users Love:**
- Label scanning convenience
- "Taste Profile" that helps them understand their own preferences
- Comprehensive database (15M+ wines)
- Community reviews feel authentic

**Key Takeaway for Nyam:** The Taste Profile is Vivino's stickiest feature — users return to see how their palate evolves. Never put core features behind a paywall after they've been free.

### 2. Untappd (Beer Social) — 10M+ users

**Profile Page Shows:**
- Total unique check-ins, total check-ins
- Badge collection (earned badges displayed prominently)
- Recent check-ins with ratings and photos
- Top-rated beers, favorite styles
- Friends list

**Most Engaging Social Features:**
- Check-in system (rate + photo + location + tasting notes)
- Badge unlocking (local, beer, venue, special badges)
- Social feed of friends' check-ins
- Venue beer menus (real-time tap lists)

**Activity Feed:**
- Chronological friend feed showing check-ins
- Each check-in = beer name + rating + photo + venue + badge earned

**Gamification (Best-in-Class):**
- 1 billion badges earned as of 2022
- Badge categories: exploration (try new styles), location (visit venues), quantity (milestones), seasonal, promotional
- Examples: "Weekday Warrior" (5 beers during weekdays), "New Brew Thursday" (new beer on 3 Thursdays)
- Badges drive exploration and content creation

**User Complaints:**
- Interface "strongly lacks user-friendly features" vs mainstream social apps
- Character limit on comments too restrictive
- No private notes option
- Rating system gamed — one-star ratings from style-aversion
- Privacy concerns (data sharing without consent)

**What Users Love:**
- Badge system is genuinely addictive
- Great travel companion (find venues in new cities)
- Beer discovery through friends' activity
- Community bonding over shared hobby

**Key Takeaway for Nyam:** Badges that encourage *exploration* (new neighborhoods, new cuisines) work much better than badges that reward *volume*. The Untappd longitudinal study (2026) warns that quantity-based gamification is ethically problematic for food/drink apps.

### 3. Beli (Restaurant Ranking) — 75M+ reviews, 80% users under 35

**Profile Page Shows:**
- Follower/following count
- Recent activity feed
- Platform rank (based on # of places rated)
- "Taste Profile" — breakdown by cuisine, city, country
- Total restaurants eaten stat
- Favorite cuisines and highest-ranked cities

**Most Engaging Social Features:**
- Comparative ranking (not star ratings — "did you like A more than B?")
- "Match Score" with friends (taste compatibility %)
- Friend feed showing recent rankings
- Two scores per restaurant: "how much YOU'd like it" + "how much your FRIENDS liked it"
- Leaderboard (city-specific and platform-wide)
- "What to Order" recommendations
- Group Dinner AI matching

**Activity Feed:**
- Social feed of friends' restaurant rankings
- Each entry shows restaurant + score + photos + dishes

**Gamification:**
- Leaderboard ranking motivates more check-ins
- Tastemakers program (NYC food experts with special badges)
- Taste Profile becomes more accurate with more data (15+ restaurants minimum)
- Streaks for ranking consistency

**User Complaints:**
- Can't rank within categories (brunch vs brunch, Italian vs Italian)
- Heavy NYC bias
- Forced comparison system sometimes feels inappropriate
- Too many features, app feels bloated
- No dark mode
- Criticism of "elite restaurant" focus and streak-chasing over genuine interest

**What Users Love:**
- Comparative ranking feels more natural than star ratings
- Friend match score is "addictive"
- Taste Profile helps discover patterns in own preferences
- Social discovery — more likely to visit places friends rave about

**Key Takeaway for Nyam:** The "Match Score" (taste compatibility between friends) is Beli's killer feature — it gives users a reason to check friends' profiles. The dual score (personal prediction + friend average) is extremely useful for decision-making.

### 4. Yelp (Restaurant Reviews) — Largest Western review platform

**Profile Page Shows:**
- Review count, photo count, friends count
- Elite badge (if applicable: Gold for 5yr, Black for 10yr)
- Collections (curated lists)
- Recent reviews, tips, check-ins
- Compliments received from other users

**Most Engaging Social Features:**
- Review writing (photos + detailed text)
- "Useful / Funny / Cool" votes on reviews
- Compliment system (user-to-user recognition)
- Collections (shareable curated lists)
- Elite Squad program
- AI-refreshed home feed with trending content

**Activity Feed:**
- Newsfeed shows friends' reviews, check-ins, tips
- Business activity tracking for owners

**Gamification:**
- Elite Squad: application-based → yearly renewal → exclusive events
- Elite badges (regular → Gold 5yr → Black 10yr → All-Star)
- Compliment system creates positive reinforcement loop
- Review milestone tracking

**User Complaints:**
- Elite system feels exclusive/gatekeeping
- Review manipulation concerns (businesses gaming reviews)
- Advertising dominance in search results
- Platform declining in relevance vs Google Maps

**What Users Love:**
- Elite events create real-world community
- Detailed reviews with photos are genuinely helpful
- Collections feature for trip planning
- Compliment system feels rewarding

**Key Takeaway for Nyam:** Yelp's Elite program proves that *exclusive real-world events* based on contribution level create the strongest community bonds. Compliments (user-to-user recognition) are an underrated engagement tool.

### 5. The Infatuation / Zagat

**Profile Features:**
- Save favorite reviews
- Curated personal lists
- Zagat Curator badge (for selected contributors)

**Social Model:**
- Curated editorial + community contributor hybrid
- "Perfect Fors" categories (Girls' Night Out, Late Night Eats)
- 88 Zagat Curators in NYC contributing reviews
- Emphasis on trusted, credible recommendations over crowd-sourcing

**Key Takeaway for Nyam:** The "Perfect For" / occasion-based categorization is highly valued by users. Curated lists from trusted members (not just algorithmic) build deeper trust.

---

### 6. Zomato (India) — Delivery + Social

**Profile Features:**
- Order history, reviews written
- Followers/following
- Food photos shared

**Social Features:**
- "Loved by Friends" tag on restaurants (shows which friends ordered there)
- Contact sync → see what friends are ordering
- Photo sharing with community
- UGC-driven reviews and ratings

**Gamification:**
- Reward points for reviews/photos
- Contributor tiers

**User Complaints:**
- "Loved by Friends" raised privacy concerns — turns private food choices into public data
- Users didn't opt in to having their orders visible to contacts

**Key Takeaway for Nyam:** Social features that expose user behavior without explicit opt-in cause backlash. Nyam's privacy-first approach (share only what you choose to) is correct.

---

### 7. Swiggy (India) — Delivery + Discovery

**Social Features:**
- "Eatlists" — playlist-style food recommendation lists (like music playlists)
- Share Eatlists via WhatsApp/Instagram
- Browse others' Eatlists (e.g., "Late-night Cravings in Mumbai")

**Key Takeaway for Nyam:** The "Eatlist" concept (themed shareable lists) is a lightweight, high-engagement social feature. Users don't need to write reviews — just curate.

---

### 8. Tabelog (Japan) — 82M+ ratings

**Profile Features:**
- Review history with detailed photos
- Reviewer reputation (weighted by review count and history)

**Social/Community:**
- 3M+ registered reviewers
- Reviewer trustworthiness weighting (long-term reviewer > tourist)
- Find reviewers who match your tastes
- No explicit follow feature — trust is signal-based

**Rating System:**
- 5-star scale but calibrated ultra-conservatively (3.5 = excellent, no restaurant has ever reached 5.0)
- Weighted by reviewer credibility

**Key Takeaway for Nyam:** Tabelog proves that *reviewer credibility weighting* is crucial for trust. Nyam's XP/level system already enables this naturally — experienced users' opinions should carry visible weight.

---

### 9. MangoPlate (Korea) — 200K+ restaurants

**Profile Features:**
- Reviews written, bookmarks, contributions
- "Holics" badge for active contributors

**Social Features:**
- Follow reviewers with similar tastes
- Facebook Login integration (+35% time spent for connected users)
- "MyList" themed collections (e.g., "My Favorite Wine Bars in Seoul")
- Filter reviews by "people I follow" vs "Holics" vs "everyone"

**Key Takeaway for Nyam:** Filtering content by relationship tier (my friends > trusted contributors > everyone) is a validated pattern. The "MyList" themed collection feature maps perfectly to Nyam's collection concept.

---

### 10. Dining Code (Korea) — Big Data Focus

**Features:**
- AI-filtered reviews (removes promotional/ad blog posts)
- Big data ranking using likes, comments, blog reputation
- AI dining schedule planner

**Key Takeaway for Nyam:** Filtering out fake/promotional content is highly valued by Korean users who are fatigued by blog-ad reviews.

---

### 11. DoorDash Zesty (NEW — Dec 2025)

**Profile Features:**
- Posts/reviews with photos
- Follower/following ("Zesties")

**Social Features:**
- AI chatbot for natural-language restaurant discovery
- Social feed of reviews from people you follow
- Simple 3-tier rating: "Loved this!" / "Kinda mid" / "Not for me"
- Conversational follow-ups ("What do most people order?")

**Key Takeaway for Nyam:** The 3-tier simple rating reduces friction dramatically. The AI-conversational discovery model is the emerging 2026 trend.

---

### 12. CellarTracker (Wine Collectors) — 13M+ tasting notes

**Profile Features:**
- Cellar inventory (detailed bottle tracking)
- Tasting notes history
- "Tasting Stories" (multi-note narratives)

**Social Features:**
- Find & follow users with similar tastes
- Public vs private notes toggle per review
- 7.5M+ community tasting notes
- Integration with wine bulletin boards

**Key Takeaway for Nyam:** The public/private toggle per note is essential — users want granular control. "Tasting Stories" (narrative format) is an interesting model for Nyam's Phase 2 AI blog reviews.

---

### 13. Delectable (Wine Social)

**Profile Features:**
- Wine scan history with ratings/notes
- Followers/following (sommeliers, critics, friends)

**Social Features:**
- Follow industry professionals (sommeliers, critics)
- Comment on posts
- Premium badge for subscribers

**Key Takeaway for Nyam:** Mixing everyday users with industry professionals in the same social graph creates aspirational engagement.

## Part 2: Cross-App Pattern Analysis

### What Makes Users Return to a Profile Page?

| Pattern | Apps Using It | Effectiveness |
|---------|--------------|---------------|
| **Evolving Taste Profile** | Vivino, Beli, CellarTracker | Very High — users check back to see how their palate/preferences change |
| **Stats dashboard** | Untappd, Beli, Yelp | High — total counts, streaks, milestones create "scorecard" motivation |
| **Badge/level collection** | Untappd, Yelp Elite | High — collection instinct drives repeat visits |
| **Match score with friends** | Beli, Vivino | High — social curiosity ("who has my taste?") |
| **Activity history** | All apps | Medium — reference/recall use case |
| **Curated lists/collections** | Beli, MangoPlate, Yelp, Swiggy | Medium — creation + sharing drives returns |

### Activity Feed vs Static Profile

| Approach | Pros | Cons | Best For |
|----------|------|------|----------|
| **Activity feed-centric** (Untappd, Beli) | Real-time engagement, FOMO, social proof | Can feel noisy, overwhelming | Discovery-oriented apps |
| **Static profile-centric** (Vivino, CellarTracker) | Clean, personal, reference-oriented | Lower daily engagement | Collection/journal apps |
| **Hybrid** (Yelp, Zomato) | Balanced — stats + feed | Complexity | Mature platforms |

**Recommendation for Nyam:** Hybrid approach — profile page is a **personal stats dashboard** (taste profile, quadrant, levels), with activity feed living in the Bubble/Home sections. This matches Nyam's "record first, social second" philosophy.

### Social Proof Elements That Work

1. **Friend endorsement** — "3 friends rated this 85+" (Beli, Zomato) → strongest conversion driver
2. **Taste match %** — "87% match with your taste" (Vivino, Beli) → reduces decision anxiety
3. **Expert/level indicator** — reviewer's relevant level shown (Tabelog, Yelp Elite) → credibility signal
4. **Photo-first reviews** — visual proof of experience (all apps) → most engaging content type
5. **Aggregate stats** — "4.2 avg from 127 reviews" → baseline expectation setting

### Community/Group Features That Succeed

| Feature | Success Factor | App Example |
|---------|---------------|-------------|
| **Small, closed groups** | Trust through intimacy | Nyam Bubbles, Facebook Groups |
| **Themed lists/collections** | Low-effort curation | MangoPlate MyList, Swiggy Eatlists |
| **Real-world meetups** | Deepest community bonds | Yelp Elite events |
| **Leaderboards (local)** | Competition within context | Beli city leaderboards |
| **Curator programs** | Aspiration + quality content | Zagat Curators, Beli Tastemakers |

### Gamification: What Actually Works

**Effective patterns:**
- Progress bars and goals (preferred by users over badges)
- Exploration badges ("try 5 different cuisines") — positive behavior nudge
- XP/level systems tied to meaningful activity
- Streaks (7+ day streaks increase daily engagement 2.3x)
- Taste Profile evolution (passive gamification — no effort needed)

**Problematic patterns:**
- Quantity-based badges (ethically questionable for food/drink — see Untappd study)
- Public leaderboards without context (creates unhealthy competition)
- Streak pressure that overrides genuine interest (Beli criticism)
- Pay-to-unlock gamification elements

**Research Data:**
- Gamification boosts engagement by 30% on average
- Social accountability increases habit completion by 65%
- 92% more spending from users in reward programs (restaurant context)
- 40-60% higher DAU with dual gamification (streaks + milestones)
- Users prefer goals/progress > badges/leaderboards

## Part 3: Recommendations for Nyam v2 Profile Page

### Current Nyam Profile (from PROFILE.md):
- Avatar, nickname, bio
- Stats: records / restaurants / wines
- Top 3 levels (neighborhood-based)
- Quadrant chart (restaurant + wine tabs)
- Privacy: bubble_only default

### Recommended Enhancements (prioritized)

#### P1 — High Impact, Aligns With Current Design

1. **Taste Profile Summary** (a la Vivino/Beli)
   - Auto-generated text: "당신은 을지로에서 캐주얼한 맛집을 찾는 탐험가입니다"
   - Already planned for Phase 3 — consider accelerating
   - This is the #1 reason users return to their own profile

2. **Stats Enhancement**
   - Add: cuisine distribution (pie chart or bar), price range distribution, most-visited neighborhoods
   - These create "aha moments" that users screenshot and share

3. **Taste Match Score** (a la Beli)
   - When viewing another user's profile: "취향 일치도 87%"
   - Based on overlapping restaurant/wine ratings
   - This is the single most viral social feature across food apps

4. **Collections/Lists Tab**
   - Themed lists users create and share ("을지로 혼밥 맛집", "데이트 와인")
   - Shareable via link (even outside app)
   - Low effort, high social value

#### P2 — Medium Impact, Worth Planning

5. **"Perfect For" Tags on Profile**
   - Show what contexts the user typically records: "혼밥 전문가", "와인 디너 애호가"
   - Auto-derived from situation tags in their records

6. **Level Progress Bar**
   - Show XP progress to next level for top 3 areas
   - Progress bars > static numbers for engagement (research-backed)

7. **Highlight Reel / Pinned Records**
   - Let users pin 3-5 records to top of profile
   - Curated self-expression (Instagram bio energy)

8. **Monthly/Yearly Wrapped**
   - Spotify-Wrapped style summary: "이번 달 12곳 기록, 새로운 동네 3곳 탐험"
   - Highly shareable, drives re-engagement

#### P3 — Long-term / Experimental

9. **Bubble Activity Summary on Profile**
   - "3개 버블 활동 중 · 이번 주 공유 5건"
   - Social proof without exposing private data

10. **Reviewer Credibility Signal**
    - When viewing others' shared records in bubbles, show relevant level
    - Already in BUBBLE.md — reinforce on profile page too

### What NOT to Do (Based on Research)

| Anti-Pattern | Why It Fails | Source |
|-------------|-------------|--------|
| Public follower count emphasis | Creates vanity metrics, anxiety | Beli/Instagram fatigue |
| Quantity-based badges ("Record 100 restaurants!") | Encourages volume over quality; ethically problematic | Untappd longitudinal study |
| Algorithmic feed on profile | Breaks trust, feels manipulative | Nyam principle already correct |
| Auto-sharing without opt-in | Privacy backlash | Zomato "Loved by Friends" controversy |
| Paywall on previously free features | Destroys trust rapidly | Vivino Premium backlash |
| Complex badge taxonomy | Overwhelming, loses meaning | Untappd badge fatigue |

### Nyam's Competitive Advantages (Already Designed)

1. **Privacy-first sharing** (explicit opt-in per record per bubble) — avoids Zomato's mistake
2. **Chronological feed, no algorithm** — builds trust that competitors are losing
3. **Closed bubbles** — creates Tabelog-level trust in a social context
4. **Quadrant chart** — unique visual identity that no competitor has
5. **Neighborhood-based XP** — more meaningful than generic "foodie level"
6. **Dual domain (food + wine)** — no competitor does both well socially
